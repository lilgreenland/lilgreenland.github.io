Newton's Laws

Newton's First law: Inertia

When viewed in an inertial reference frame, an object either remains at rest or continues to move at a constant velocity, unless acted upon by an external force.

  • Objects keep moving in a straight line at a constant velocity unless they have a force acting on them.
  • Inertia is the resistance of any physical object to any change in speed or direction.



    • Newton's Second law: Force

    The vector sum of the external forces F on an object is equal to the mass m of that object multiplied by the acceleration vector of the object.

    ΣF = ma

    F = force [N, Newtons, kg m/s2] vector
    A force is something that will try to accelerate an object. Like a push or a pull.

    Σ = sigma The greek letter Sigma represents the summation of numbers to find the total. In this equation it means add up all the forces to get a total force.

    F1+F2+F3+F4 + ... = ma

    m = mass [kg, kilogram]
    Mass is a measure of an object's resistance to acceleration when a force is applied.

    a = acceleration [m/s2] vector
    Acceleration is the change in velocity over time.




    Example: On a corner kick you use the Magnus Effect curve the ball into the goal. To approximate the magnus effect assume the ball has a net force of 1N towards the goal. How much acceleration does that produce on a 0.43 kg soccerball? How much does the ball deviate from it's original path after 1.5 seconds?

    solution $$F=ma$$ $$\frac{F}{m}=a$$ $$\frac{1}{0.43}=a$$ $$2.33\small\frac{m}{s^{2}}=\normalsize a$$

    \(a=2.33\), \(\Delta t=1.5\), \(\Delta x=?\), \(v_{i}=0\)

    $$\Delta x = v_{i}\Delta t + \small\frac{1}{2}a \Delta t^{2}$$ $$\Delta x = 0(1.5) + \small\frac{1}{2}(2.33) (1.5)^{2}$$ $$\Delta x = 2.62 m$$



    Newton's Third law: equal and Opposite Forces

    When one body exerts a force on a second body, the second body simultaneously exerts a force equal in magnitude and opposite in direction on the first body.
