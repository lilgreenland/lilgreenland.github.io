Momentum

Momentum is the product of mass and velocity.

p = mv

p = momentum [kg m/s] vector
m = mass [kg]
v = velocity[m/s] vector




Conservation of Momentum

  • Momentum is an important concept in physics because the total momentum for a system of objects is conserved. This means the sum of all momentums will be the same before a collision and after.

  • Click the simulation above a few times and note that the total momentum is the same before and after a collision.

  • To use conservation of momentum we pick two points in time and set the sum of the total momentums equal to each other.

    • initial momentum = final momentum  
    p1 + p2 + ... = p1 + p2 + ...
    m1u1 + m2u2 + ... = m1v1 + v2m2 + ...

  • To keep track of before and after velocity we will use u for velocity before and v for velocity after.






    • Example: A green and white box collide. The green box has a mass of 2kg and is moving to the right at 3m/s. The white box is moving to the left at 1m/s and has a mass of 10kg. If the white box has stopped after the collision what is the velocity of the green box?

    solution

    green + white = green + white

    $$m_{1}u_{1}+m_{2}u_{2}=m_{1}v_{1}+m_{2}v_{2}$$ $$(2)(3)+(10)(-1)=(2)v_{1}+(1)(0)$$ $$6-10=(2)v_{1}$$ $$-4=(2)v_{1}$$ $$-2=v_{1}$$
