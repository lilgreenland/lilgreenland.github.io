To Calculate average velocity and average acceleration we can always use these equations:

$$v_{avg} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$$

$$a_{avg} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$$


The Equations of Motion

If an object's acceleration is constant the equations of motion are also true. Here is the derivation of these equations.

$$v_{f} = v_{i}+a \Delta t$$ $$\Delta x = v_{i}\Delta t + \small\frac{1}{2}a \Delta t^{2}$$ $$\Delta x = \small\frac{1}{2}\normalsize(v_{f}+v_{i})\Delta t$$ $$v_{f}^{2} = v_{i}^{2}+2a \Delta x$$ $$\Delta x = v_{f}\Delta t - \small\frac{1}{2}a \Delta t^{2}$$

To choose which equation to use write down the one unknown and all the known variables. Match your variables to one of the equations above.



Example: A bullet aimed straight up leaves the barrel of a gun at 400m/s. It accelerates down at -9.8m/s2. If the bullet travels for 40.8s before stopping how far up did it go? (In the real world the bullet would stop quicker because of air friction.)

\(\Delta t = 40.8s\) \(\Delta x = 16320 - 8157\)
\(\Delta x = ?\) \(\Delta x = 8163m\)

solution

Friction...

Objects on earth tend to slow down because of friction. Air friction is hard to simplify with math. Contact friction is more predictable so we will learn that next unit. For now assume objects are in a vacuum and ignore all friction.

Gravity

On earth everything is pulled down at 9.8 m/s2. This rate is the same for cars, birds, puppies, apples, and balloons. Acceleration from gravity changes based on the mass of the planet and your distance to the center of the planet.
